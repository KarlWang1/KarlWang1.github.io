Project Summary

2020-09-05

My project brought a new method of a robot design that can automatically solve Rubik’s cube faster than traditional design. I find that most of the current Rubik's Cube robots are using the structure of dual-arm and two-finger. It may take more steps than arms with multi-finger. I brought up a novel method of four fingers robot which could process several rotate operations parallelly. After a set of experiments, the novel method can save half of the steps of rotation per time than the traditional methods. As for the traditional two-finger robot, there are only two surfaces can be rotated directly, while the other four surfaces require more steps to operate the rotation. In the scheme of four-finger design, the rotate operation of the front, rear, left, and right sides of the cube are logically equivalent to the bottom surface which narrows the average speed of rotation for each side. Due to the scheme of four-finger design, many logical operations can be processed in parallel, which decreases the number of steps of operation.

To guarantee a perfect performance of recognization, this project brought up an algorithm base on K-means clustering. Firstly, this program divides all the input colors into 6 categories which can overcome the vibrancy in the camera recording and of the blemish of the magic. Also, I have designed a special procession logic based on the famous Two-phase algorithm which can increase the "width" and "depth" of its search process and finding the time-optimal the solution to improve the speed of solving the Rubik's Cube.

To accomplish the novel design, this project used the closed-loop PID method to control the high-torque brushless DC motors (RM6623 motors and M2006 motors produced by DJI), accomplishing the wrist rotation and finger grasp accurately. According to a set of experiments, the new scheme I brought up can save half of the steps of rotation per time than the two-finger design.
